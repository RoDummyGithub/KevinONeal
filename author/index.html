AUTHOR

Kevin O'Neal

Kevin O’neal writes this story about places and groups of people he knew. The story takes primarily on the Upper Orchard section of Levittown, PA, where he was a mailman. Since he was a young man, Kevin was fascinated with the police force and military. O’neal enlisted in the US Navy from 1988 through 1994. His primary duties were as Payroll Clerk and Damage Control Petty Officer. Kevin O’neal stood an assortment of watches on duty days. After several years out, Kevin enlisted in the New Jersey National Guard from 1996 through 1999. He was schooled in infantry battle tactics, ambush, and hot to defeat an ambush. Kevin O’neal was qualified on the M16 rifle, M50 and M60 machine guns and his favorite weapon is the M203.

Kevin O’neal feels for all veterans. Especially the Vietnam veterans who didn’t get the respect they deserve until years after the war. Love, sex, action, religion and depression all play a role in the Bordowski Household.

Kevin worked at a Bucks County Hospital where he met some Vietnam veterans. He heard many stories. Between the years of 1979 through 1981 he dealt with many mental patients and saw addiction from sex to drugs and alcohol through many depressions.

Kevin O’neal is currently a non-practicing Roman Catholic whose last mass Midnight Mass 1991. He left peacefully parting ways, Kevin’s religious views are most seen in Dallas Bordowski.
